# Modern-Physics-Research-Papers
This section contains the work performed about Modern Physics in the spring semester at Laguardia Comunity College

## Optics and Special Relativity Lab Report
This work explores key concepts in modern physics, including optical interference, the double-slit experiment, and special relativity. It focuses on reproducing and analyzing these phenomena through laboratory experiments and simulations, comparing results with theoretical predictions. Additionally, it presents experimental proposals related to the Michelson–Morley experiment and relativistic time dilation using laser-based measurements, highlighting the connection between theory and observation in fundamental physics.

## Quantum Physics Simulations Using the PhET Interactive Platform
This study uses PhET simulations to explore key quantum mechanics concepts, including the hydrogen atom model, quantum measurement probability, semiconductor behavior in a p–n junction, and quantum tunneling. The results illustrate quantized energy levels, probabilistic outcomes, and wave-function behavior in different potential regions. Overall, the simulations highlight fundamental principles of quantum mechanics and their applications in atomic and semiconductor physics.

## Experimental Determination of Thermal Expansion via Michelson Interferometry
This work presents an experimental study of thermal expansion using a Michelson interferometer. By analyzing interference fringe shifts produced during temperature changes, the coefficient of thermal expansion of different materials is determined with high precision. The study highlights the advantages of optical interferometry for measuring nanometer-scale length variations and demonstrates the relationship between thermal effects and optical interference in precision metrology.
